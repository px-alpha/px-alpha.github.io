What happened to the single source of the truth?

What happened to the single source of the truth?

Introduction

We’ve all been there. The single source of the truth (or SSOTT). In the nineties, or possibly still in the early part of this century. And of course it’s appealing: make sure that data is stored only once, so you never have to worry about data consistency, synchronization and what not. And when you can, you should. Have it I mean. It still is the best option if it is available in your specific situation.

An inconvenient truth

In many cases SSOTT is simply not possible anymore. A typical situation is IT environments that result from corporate mergers. Often the source IT systems are that complex that merging the systems of both merging companies is simply too complex to be a viable alternative. Another reasons is the current agility of the IT landscape that results from cloud adoption and low-code/no-code application development tools. Yet another reason why the SSOTT is not achieved originates in the opposite of agility: the aging of the IT landscape: companies implement new tools that overlap functionality wise with older tools, just to stay competitive. These partly overlapping tools also create more than one source of the truth.

Apart from accepting the fact of multiple sources of the truth (MSOTT) and apart from the obvious technical solution of implementing a service bus solution to solve that problem, one sometimes sees that a radical decision is made to use only one the systems of the merging groups, to stay close to the SSOTT principle. That choice is often quite costly as well, especially if the other systems that are not the chosen ones, contain unique and valuable functionality. Which they often do.

Another situation where MSOTT is almost unavoidable is in IT based design factories, typical for capital projects. There a multitude of applications are used. Basic design and detailed design for example usually still require different tools to stay competitive in the market. Another reason the often met requirement of data delivery in the clients source system format. If the supplier decides to continue to use their own design tooling for the majority of the work, often a MSOTT situation is unavoidable.

Multiple sourced data: a reality

Given the fact that MSOTT data is there, and will be there for some time in the future: apart from SSOTT solutions we (architects, system designers, integration specialist, working in the capital project environment) need to have the MSOTT tooling in our belt as well. Possibly as an in between solution, but probably one that will be here some time.
