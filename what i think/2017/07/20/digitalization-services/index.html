Digitalization

In general, digitization is defined along the lines of ‘…integration of digital technologies in everyday life..’ (see: http://www.businessdictionary.com/definition/digitalization.html). This is also my main view point for digitalization.

To generalize many other existing definitions a bit, digitalization is the process in which modern digital technologies are applied to existing business processes resulting in significant changes in the way value is created.

What makes digitalization so complex is that it is not only technology. To be successful, changes in business processes and business culture are required as well. In general, digitalization is a transformation in more than one area.
What this blog is all about: project information

And by definition: in capital projects the process involves the project parties, meaning the common understaning must be achieved over several seperate companies. In future articles an approach will be sketched that makes this process easier.

Being involved in these kind of changes, I’ve learned that in capital projects information integration is a key digitalization aspect. Integration inside the corporation or business unit, but especially between businesses and companies.

There are three reasons why integration is so valuable. Firstly the integration helps to break down the barriers between departments, functional areas and data silos. Secondly, the resulting integrated information can be used for analyses and improvement of data quality. Thirdly the integration of data between companies (by, for example, exchanging linked datasets, or using shared linked data resources) helps to ensure a common understanding of shared data which accellerates project execution.

Personally, I think that the actual sharing of data and information often is the key to successful digitalization initiatives. The modern technology, the increased competition and expected reduction in lead time and profit from sharing early in a controlled way.
